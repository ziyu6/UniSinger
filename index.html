1. Abstract

While Song Generation and Singing Voice Conversion (SVC) have witnessed significant advancements, they have long been developed under isolated paradigms: the former lacks zero-shot timbre cloning capabilities, while the latter overlooks the acoustic synergy between vocals and accompaniment. To bridge this gap, we propose the first end-to-end framework unifying Timbre-Controllable Song Generation and Accompaniment-Coordinated SVC. Building upon the Multimodal Diffusion Transformer (MMDiT) architecture, we construct a cross-task Unified Vocal Timbre Space. By seamlessly transferring disentangled timbre features from SVC task to Song Generation task, this design endows the latter with unprecedented zero-shot timbre cloning capabilities. Furthermore, to mitigate multi-task optimization conflicts, we design a Progressive Curriculum Training strategy. By employing Task-Specific Modality Masking to construct information bottlenecks, we guide the model to progressively decouple and master the generative mechanics of semantic content, vocal timbre, and accompaniment. Experimental results demonstrate that our framework not only achieves State-of-The-Art performance on foundational tasks but also realizes breakthrough capabilities in Timbre-Controllable Song Generation and Accompaniment-Coordinated SVC, offering new possibilities for intelligent music production.
